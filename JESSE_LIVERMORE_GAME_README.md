## ðŸŽ¯ What is Short Selling?

Short selling is betting that a stock price will **go down**:

1. **Borrow** shares from your broker
2. **Sell** them immediately at current market price
3. **Wait** for price to drop
4. **Buy back** shares at lower price
5. **Return** shares to broker
6. **Keep** the difference as profit

**Example:**
- Borrow 1,000 shares at $30 â†’ Sell for à¸¿30,000

## ðŸ¦ The House: Your Brokerage

### Who Is "The House"?

**The House** represents your brokerage firm (like Interactive Brokers, TD Ameritrade, Fidelity, etc.). They act as:

1. **Share Locator** - Finds shares available to borrow
2. **Lender** - Lends you the shares for shorting
3. **Risk Manager** - Monitors your margin and liquidates if necessary
4. **Fee Collector** - Charges borrow fees and commissions

### Where Do Borrowed Shares Come From?

The broker locates shares from three main sources:

#### 1. **Their Own Inventory**
- The brokerage holds shares in their own account
- Easiest to locate, lowest borrow fees

#### 2. **Other Clients' Margin Accounts** (Most Common)
- When you open a margin account, the fine print allows the broker to lend your shares
- Client A holds 10,000 BMNR shares long
- Broker lends those shares to Client B for shorting
- Client A usually doesn't know and still sees their shares in the account
- If Client A tries to sell, broker must "recall" shares from Client B

#### 3. **Institutional Share Lending Programs**
- Pension funds, mutual funds, ETFs lend shares to earn extra income
- Broker borrows from these institutions and re-lends to you
- Higher fees for "hard-to-borrow" stocks

## ðŸ“– Who Was Jesse Livermore?

**Jesse Lauriston Livermore (1877-1940)**

- One of the most famous traders in history
- Made $100 million shorting before the **1929 stock market crash** (worth ~$1.7B today)
- Known as the "Great Bear of Wall Street"
- Made and lost several fortunes throughout his career
- Popularized tape reading and technical analysis
- His book *"Reminiscences of a Stock Operator"* (1923) is still required reading for traders

**His Famous Trades:**
- 1907 Panic: Made $3 million shorting (J.P. Morgan personally asked him to stop)
- 1929 Crash: Made $100 million in one day shorting the market
- Final Years: Lost most of his fortune in bad trades during the 1930s

**His Legacy:**
> *"The game taught me the game. And it will never be finished. I may take a summer vacation somewhere, but I always come back to the game. It's in my blood."*
